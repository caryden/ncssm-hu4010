How to Slice the Market

  • Demographics: Age, location, income, education
  • Psychographics: Values, attitudes, lifestyle
  • Behavioral: Usage patterns, benefits sought
  • Situational: Context, trigger events

Your Tool: The Segmentation Matrix

Rate each segment on key criteria to find your best opportunity

Criterion 1: Problem Intensity

How much does it hurt?

"Hair on fire" beats "mild inconvenience"

Criterion 2: Accessibility

Can you reach them?

The best customers are ones you can actually talk to.

Criterion 3: Market Size

Big enough to matter, small enough to win

Step 2: Select Your Beachhead

Your First Landing Zone

Like D-Day: Win one beach first, then expand into Europe.

The Allies didn't try to invade everywhere at once.

The Case for Focus

  • Limited resources demand focus
  • Different segments need different everything
  • Word-of-mouth spreads within segments
  • Domination creates momentum
  • You can expand later (but not if you die first)

Going narrow feels risky.
Going broad is actually risky.

The Four Tests

Winnable

Can you become #1 in this segment?

Valuable

Is it worth winning?

Strategic

Does winning here unlock more?

Achievable

Can you actually reach them?

All four must be YES.

Remember

Segmentation Research Methods

Different methods to gather data for your segmentation matrix:

  • Surveys: Quick quantitative data from many potential customers
  • Interviews: Deep qualitative insights from 1-on-1 conversations
  • Observation: Watch how potential customers behave in natural settings
  • Secondary Research: Industry reports, census data, existing studies

Common Segmentation Mistakes

Too Broad

"Millennials who like technology"

Problem: No actionable insights

Too Narrow

"Left-handed accountants in Toledo who own cats"

Problem: Not enough customers

Demographics Only

"Women aged 25-35"

Problem: Ignores behavior and needs

No Pain Point

"People who might be interested"

Problem: No urgency to buy

Segmentation Matrix Template

Use this scoring system (1-5) for each criterion:

Criterion 1 (Low) 5 (High)
Problem Intensity Mild annoyance Hair on fire
Accessibility Hard to find Easy to reach
Market Size Too small Large enough
Competition Saturated Underserved
Personal Fit No connection Deep expertise

Total possible score: 25. Your beachhead should score at least 18.
