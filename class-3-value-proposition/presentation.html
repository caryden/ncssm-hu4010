Step 6: Full Life Cycle Use Case

Your Product Doesn't Exist in Isolation

  • Customers experience you in context of their whole day
  • Every touchpoint is an opportunity (or failure point)
  • Understanding the journey reveals where to add value
  • Competitors might beat you in phases you forgot about

8 Phases of the Customer Journey

  • Awareness: How do they discover you?
  • Consideration: How do they evaluate options?
  • Acquisition: How do they buy/sign up?
  • Onboarding: First experience with your product
  • Usage: The core experience (repeat)

Journey Phases (continued)

  • Support: When things go wrong
  • Renewal: Will they come back?
  • Churn: Why do they leave?

Most founders focus only on Usage. Don't make that mistake.

Step 7: High-Level Product Spec

The Brochure Test

Could you show this to a customer and they'd understand what you're offering in 30 seconds?

What It's NOT

  • Technical architecture diagrams
  • Database schemas
  • Code specifications
  • 50-page feature lists

What It IS

  • Visual representation of the experience
  • Core features that deliver value
  • Simple enough to explain in 2 minutes
  • "Brochure test" ready

The 10x Rule Deep Dive

Why 10x? Because the switching cost for customers is real.

Switching Costs Include:

  • Learning a new system (time)
  • Migrating data (effort)
  • Risk of failure (fear)
  • Team buy-in (politics)
  • Breaking habits (psychology)

The Math: If switching costs = 20% of value, and you want 3x ROI after switching, you need: Value = 3x √ó (Price + 0.2 √ó Value) ‚Üí Solve for Value ‚âà 10x Price
