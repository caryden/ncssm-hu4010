Step 16: Set Your Pricing Framework

Three Pricing Approaches

  • Cost-Plus: Your costs + markup (floor)
  • Competitive: Based on alternatives (context)
  • Value-Based: Based on value delivered (ceiling)

Value-based pricing captures the most opportunity.

The 10x Rule Revisited

If you deliver $1,000 of value, you can charge $100

Customers need to feel they're getting a deal

Pricing Psychology

Behavioral principles that influence pricing decisions

Anchoring

First price seen becomes the reference point. Show the expensive option first.

Decoy Effect

Add a third option to make the target option look better. The "Pro" tier strategy.

Charm Pricing

$9.99 vs $10 ‚Äî left-digit effect makes prices feel significantly lower.

Price-Quality Signal

Higher prices can signal higher quality. Don't underprice premium products.

Loss Aversion

Frame pricing around what they lose by not buying, not just what they gain.

Social Proof

"Most popular" labels leverage herd behavior to drive tier selection.
