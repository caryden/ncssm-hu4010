1. Hook (30 seconds)

Grab attention immediately

❓
Provocative question
📊
Surprising statistic
📖
Brief story
Example: "What if I told you half your homework time is wasted?"

2-3. Problem & Solution

Problem (60s)

  • Make it vivid and relatable
  • Use your persona by name
  • Quantify the pain
  • Why current solutions fail

Solution (60s)

  • One sentence what you do
  • How it works (simple)
  • Demo or visual if possible
  • Key differentiator

4-5. Market & Business Model

Market (60s)

  • TAM (with methodology)
  • Beachhead strategy
  • Expansion path
  • Why now?

Business Model (60s)

  • How you charge
  • Pricing
  • Unit economics (LTV/COCA)

6. Traction (60 seconds)

Prove it's not just an idea

💬 Customer Conversations
Use actual quotes!
🧪 Tests Run
Results and learnings
👥 Early Users
Signups or commitments
🔄 Pivots
What you learned and changed

This is where the semester's work pays off!

7-8. Team & Ask

Team (30s)

  • Why you can execute
  • Relevant skills/experience
  • Brief, confident

Ask & Close (60s)

  • What you need next (specific!)
  • Clear next steps
  • Memorable closing
  • Return to your hook

Pitch Like a Pro

⚡ Start strong—first 30 seconds matter most
📖 Tell stories, not statistics
👀 Show, don't just tell (demos > descriptions)
🔢 Use specific numbers ($500K, not "big market")
🎯 Nail the close—it's what they remember

Every team member must speak.

Divide sections. Practice handoffs.

Anticipate Tough Questions

What if a competitor copies you?
How do you know customers will pay?
Why can YOU do this?
What's your biggest risk?

Listen fully. It's okay to say:
"We don't know yet, but here's how we'll find out..."

Appendix B

Scaling Frameworks

Bowling Pin Strategy

Like knocking down bowling pins: hit the head pin (beachhead) first, and it knocks down adjacent pins. Each market victory enables the next.

🎳

Land & Expand

Start small within an organization (land), then expand to more users/departments. Common in B2B SaaS. Slack, Dropbox, Zoom all used this.

🌱

Network Effects

Product becomes more valuable as more people use it. Focus on one network first (geographic, vertical) before expanding.

🔗

Franchise Model

Prove the model works, document everything, then enable others to replicate. Works for service businesses and physical products.

📈
