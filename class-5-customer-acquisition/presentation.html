Theme 3: How Does Your Customer Acquire Your Product?

The person who uses your product is often not the person who decides to buy it.

The Perfect Setup

  • Amazing product demo
  • Enthusiastic user feedback
  • Verbal commitment from champion
  • Everyone loves it

Then IT Security said no.

3 months of work. Dead.

You can't sell to someone you don't know exists.

Map the Decision-Making Unit.

Your persona is usually the End User.

But they may not control the budget.

A teacher loves your product... but the principal approves purchases, and IT must sign off.

Champion

Your internal advocate who believes in your solution

Cares about: Looking good, having tools to sell internally, success metrics

Economic Buyer

Controls the budget and makes the financial decision

Cares about: ROI, risk mitigation, strategic alignment

Technical Buyer

Evaluates fit with existing systems

Cares about: Security, integration complexity, maintenance burden

Each Role Has Different Concerns

  • End User: "Will this make my job easier?"
  • Economic Buyer: "Will this save/make us money?"
  • Technical Buyer: "Will this integrate safely?"
  • Legal/Compliance: "Does this meet our requirements?"

You need to address ALL of these.

B2B vs B2C

B2C

  • Decision: Minutes to days
  • Stakeholders: 1-3 people
  • Sale type: Self-service
  • Risk tolerance: Higher

B2B

  • Decision: Weeks to months
  • Stakeholders: 5-15 people
  • Sale type: Sales-assisted
  • Risk tolerance: Lower

Know which world you're in.

3-6 months

Theme 4: How do you make money?

DMU Role Details

Champion: The person who will sell your product internally when you're not in the room. They need ammunition: ROI data, case studies, success metrics.
Veto Power: Often legal, security, or procurement. They rarely say yes, but can always say no. Identify them early and address their concerns proactively.
Influencer: May not have formal authority but shapes opinions. Could be respected peers, consultants, or industry analysts.

Typical B2B Sales Cycle Stages

Week 1-2
Discovery call, initial demo
Week 3-4
Technical evaluation, stakeholder meetings
Week 5-8
Proof of concept, pilot
Week 9-12
Security review, legal review
Week 13+
Contract negotiation, procurement

Building a Champion Strategy

Your champion is your most valuable asset in B2B sales.

How to Identify Champions:

  • They're frustrated with the current solution
  • They have something to gain from your success
  • They have influence within the organization
  • They're willing to spend time helping you navigate

How to Enable Champions:

  • Give them clear ROI calculations
  • Provide internal presentation materials
  • Coach them on handling objections
  • Keep them informed of deal progress
