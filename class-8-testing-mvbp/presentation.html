Steps 22-23

Define MVBP & Prove It

MVP vs MVBP

MVP

  • Minimum product that works
  • Tests "can we build it?"
  • May be free
  • Validates technology

MVBP

  • Minimum product people PAY for
  • Tests "will they BUY it?"
  • Requires transaction
  • Validates business

MVBP is the real goal.

The MVBP Test

VALUE
PAYMENT
FEEDBACK
MVBP

All three required: Customer gets value, pays money, you get feedback

Famous MVBPs

Dropbox
Video + waitlist
Proved demand existed
Zappos
Photos + manual fulfillment
Proved online shoe buying
Groupon
Blog + PDF coupons
Proved local deals work
Airbnb
Founders' apartment
Proved stranger stays

None of these were fully built products.

You haven't validated until strangers pay you real money.

The Validation Ladder

Define Your MVBP

Our MVBP is [what]
that [delivers this value]
for [this price]
and teaches us [this feedback]

Example: "Our MVBP is a manual tutoring matching service that connects students with tutors for $10/session and teaches us which subjects are most requested"

Testing Methods Deep Dive

When to use each testing technique

Landing Page

Create a simple page describing your value proposition

Best for: Testing message resonance, early demand signal

Customer Interviews

Talk to potential customers about their problems

Best for: Problem validation, understanding pain depth

Smoke Test / Fake Door

Create a buy button that tracks clicks but doesn't work

Best for: Testing willingness to pay, feature interest

Wizard of Oz

Manually deliver what seems automated to the customer

Best for: Complex tech validation, service design

Prototype

Build a simplified, non-functional version

Best for: UX validation, gathering specific feedback

Concierge MVP

Deliver the full service manually to first customers

Best for: Learning the process, high-value early customers

Assumption Categories

Questions to uncover hidden assumptions

Customer Assumptions

  • Do they actually have this problem?
  • How painful is it for them?
  • Are they actively seeking solutions?
  • Can we reach them efficiently?

Value Assumptions

  • Does our solution actually solve it?
  • Is the improvement meaningful?
  • Can we deliver consistently?
  • Is it better than alternatives?

Business Assumptions

  • Will they pay this price?
  • Can we acquire customers cheaply?
  • Will they stay (retention)?
  • Can we scale operations?
