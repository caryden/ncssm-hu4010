## How to Use This Reference

1. **Start with subject experts**: Find the channels most relevant to your topic
2. **Search within channels**: Use "site:youtube.com/c/{channel} {topic}"
3. **Check playlists**: Many channels organize content by topic
4. **Note timestamps**: Mark specific moments that demonstrate techniques
5. **Cross-reference**: Same concept explained differently helps identify misconceptions

---

## Evaluation Criteria

When selecting sources, prioritize:

1. **Visual clarity**: Are explanations supported by good visuals?
2. **Misconception awareness**: Does the creator address common mistakes?
3. **Scaffolding**: Is complexity introduced gradually?
4. **Accuracy**: Is the content factually correct?
5. **Engagement**: Will students find it interesting?
6. **Length**: Can relevant segments be extracted?

---

## Adding New Sources

When you discover a valuable source during research:

1. Verify the creator's credentials/expertise
2. Check for consistent quality across videos
3. Note their teaching style and approach
4. Add to this reference if broadly applicable
